Simulating the Poisson Limit Theorem

The goal of this assignment is to understand how continuous-time stochastic processes emerge from discrete random trials. We bridge the gap between the Binomial distribution and the Poisson Point Process.

1. Theoretical Framework

The Setup

We wish to simulate events occurring at a rate $\lambda$ over a time interval $T$. We approximate this by discretizing the time into $n$ small bins of width $\Delta t = T/n$.

In each bin, we perform a Bernoulli trial (a coin flip) where:

  • $1$ (Success/Event) occurs with probability $p = \frac{\lambda}{n}$.
  • $0$ (Failure/No Event) occurs with probability $1 - p$.

Let $N(t)$ be the number of events that have occurred up to time $t$. This is a Counting Process.

The Poisson Limit: As $n \to \infty$, the Binomial distribution $B(n, \lambda/n)$ converges to the Poisson distribution with parameter $\lambda$. The resulting continuous-time process is a Homogeneous Poisson Point Process.
